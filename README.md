## 📄 Project Overview  
This project investigates the perceptions of pedestrians and bicyclists regarding autonomous vehicles (AVs) in Pittsburgh. The study explores key issues such as safety, trust, and infrastructure sufficiency by analyzing survey data from local residents.  

The primary goal is to guide the development of inclusive transportation systems that ensure the safety and comfort of vulnerable road users.  

---

## 🚀 Features  
- Analysis of AV perceptions among pedestrians and cyclists.  
- Exploration of demographic factors influencing AV safety perceptions.  
- Regression modeling and data visualization using Python and R.  
- AWS implementation for scalable data processing.  

---

---

## 📊 Dataset Description  
The dataset, **"Autonomous Vehicle Survey of Bicyclists and Pedestrians in Pittsburgh"**, collects feedback from non-motorized road users on AV interactions.  
It includes:  
- Demographic data (age, gender, prior experiences)  
- Comfort and safety ratings with AVs  
- Opinions on infrastructure (bike lanes, pedestrian crossings)  
- Trust levels and willingness to share trip data  

---

## 📖 Research Questions  
1. What factors influence the comfort and safety of pedestrians and cyclists around AVs?  
2. How do demographic variables (age, experience) shape AV perceptions?  
3. Can AV safety perceptions be predicted by demographics and infrastructure?  
4. Is there a link between AV-related news familiarity and willingness to share trip data?
